Microcontrollers Overview

Microcontrollers are compact integrated circuits that contain a processor core, memory, and input/output peripherals on a single chip.They are commonly used in embedded systems to control various devices and appliances, offering real-time processing capabilities in applications ranging from consumer electronics to industrial automation

Key Features and Applications

Microcontrollers are characterized by their low cost, low power consumption, and versatility, making them ideal for applications where space and resources are limited.They find widespread use in products such as smartphones, home appliances, automotive systems, medical devices, and IoT (Internet of Things) devices, enabling efficient control and interaction with the physical world.
